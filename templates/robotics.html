Robotics Club

The Robotics Club is designed for students who are interested in designing, building, and programming robots. This club encourages creativity, problem-solving, teamwork, and hands-on technical skills in robotics and automation.

Joining Requirements:
  • Students must appear for a small entrance exam or practical test to assess their basic technical and logical skills.
  • Basic understanding of electronics, programming, or mechanical concepts is recommended to join this club.
  • Commitment to attend weekly workshops, build projects, and participate in competitions.
Selection Process:
  • In second year there should a selection process for GCC not in first year
  • Students must first take a short test to evaluate their understanding of robotics fundamentals.
  • Students who pass the test will be invited for a personal interview and hands-on assessment.
  • The interview and assessment evaluate technical knowledge, problem-solving skills, and teamwork abilities.
  • Only students who successfully complete both the test and interview will be accepted into the Robotics Club.
  • In this there is a exam it is based upon python basics and basic codes
Learning Goals:
  • Learn the basics of robotics: sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, and circuits.
  • Understand programming for robotics using Python, Arduino, or other platforms.
  • Develop problem-solving, project management, and design skills.
  • Participate in robotics competitions and hackathons organized by the club.
Uses of Robotics Club:
  • Gain hands-on experience in building and programming robots.
  • Improve performance in technical exams, projects, and competitions.
  • Collaborate with peers on innovative robotics projects.
  • Develop confidence in technical interviews and engineering challenges.
Future Benefits:
  • Enhances career opportunities in robotics, automation, AI, and mechanical/electrical engineering.
  • Builds a foundation for advanced robotics projects and competitions.
  • Opens doors to internships, certifications, and research projects in robotics and technology.
  • Develops problem-solving, teamwork, creativity, and leadership skills.
