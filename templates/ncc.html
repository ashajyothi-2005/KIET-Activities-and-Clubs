National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is a youth development movement aimed at developing character, comradeship, discipline, a secular outlook, and ideals of selfless service among young citizens. Students participate in military training and various community service activities.

By Joining NCC:
  • Serve the nation in a disciplined and responsible manner.
  • Learn leadership qualities and teamwork.
  • Improve physical fitness and mental strength.
  • Participate in social service activities and contribute to nation-building.
  • Develop a sense of unity and national integration.
NCC Selections
  • Every year, NCC conducts selections in schools and colleges. Interested students need to apply and go through:
  • Physical Fitness Test (basic running, push-ups, stamina check)
  • Drill Test (marching and basic commands)
  • Interview/Screening (to check confidence, communication, and interest in NCC)
  • Only those who show discipline, fitness, and dedication are selected to become cadets.
Types of NCC Wings

NCC in India is divided into three main wings:

1. Army Wing (NCC – Senior Division/Junior Division, e.g., 3A, 18A Battalions)
  • Training: Cadets are trained in parade, map reading, rifle shooting, obstacle training, and military tactics.
  • Camps: They attend camps like ATC, CATC, RDC, and NIC.
  • Opportunities: Cadets can appear for the ‘C’ Certificate exam, which gives preference in Army recruitment and SSB interviews.
  • Battalion Numbers (like 3A, 18A): These are different NCC Army Battalions attached to specific regions/colleges.
2. Naval Wing (NCC Navy)
  • Training: Cadets learn seamanship, boat pulling, sailing, ship modeling, navigation, and communication.
  • Camps: Naval cadets get opportunities to participate in sailing regattas, sea training camps, and naval ship visits.
  • Opportunities: Preference for recruitment in the Indian Navy and maritime careers. Some cadets also represent India in Youth Exchange Programs.
3. Air Wing (NCC Air Force)
  • Training: Cadets are trained in aviation basics, aircraft recognition, aero-modeling, flying, and navigation.
  • Flying Training: Selected cadets get hands-on experience with microlight aircraft flying.
  • Camps: Air Force cadets can attend Air Wing Training Camps and specialized aviation programs.
  • Opportunities: Preference in joining the Indian Air Force as a pilot, ground duty officer, or technical branch.
Extra Benefits of Joining NCC
  • Builds discipline, confidence, and leadership skills.
  • Special quota in defense services (Army, Navy, Air Force).
  • Adds value to college admissions and job opportunities.
  • Chance to represent India at Youth Exchange Programs abroad.
  • Lifelong learning through social service, adventure, and patriotism.
Usage of NCC in Future (Job-Oriented Process):

Being a part of NCC can significantly enhance career opportunities in government and private sectors.

1. Defense Services:

NCC cadets with a 'C' Certificate get direct entry benefits in Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force.

2. Government Jobs:

NCC cadets get preference in central and state government jobs.

3. Private Sector:

Many companies value the leadership, discipline, and time management skills gained from NCC.

4. Higher Education:

Some universities provide NCC cadets with reservation or bonus marks during admissions.
